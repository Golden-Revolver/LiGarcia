What Are Fossil Fuels?

According to National Geographic (2022), fossil fuels are the product of dead plants and animals that have pressurized underground for millions of years. When they died, some of their energy was stored, and through immense heat and pressure, this energy became compactified into the 3 fossil fuels we know of today: Coal, oil, and gas.

COAL

Coal is mostly made up of fossilized plants and can be found buried in layers of sedimentary rock. Through a long process known as carbonization, these ancient plants are slowly crushed into ever denser rocks of coal. In general, the higher the carbonization, the more energy can be harnessed from the coal. Unfortunately, mining coal can be dangerous as miners are usually exposed to high levels of toxic dust. Moreover, the carbon that plants capture is also stored in coal. When burned, this carbon can escape and release harmful greenhouse gases into the environment.

OIL

Oil, also called petroleum, is made out of dead plants and animals that have been heated and pressurized below ground. Oil deposits can usually be found in rock formations, under the ocean floor, or even on the surface where oil bubbles form. On land, drill rigs allow oil to be produced 24/7, making it a reliable source of energy for most. On sea, oil platforms are constructed to facilitate the extraction of oil from our oceans. Once refined, oil can be used for many things like providing fuel for your car. But, just like coal, burning gasoline releases stored up carbon into the atmosphere.

GAS

Similar to oil, gas (or natural gas) is formed when ancient species decompose over millions of years. The main difference being that natural gas is primarily made out of methane; another potent greenhouse gas. However, deposits of natural gas are spread thinly in layers of rock, forcing companies to use a process known as hydraulic fracturing or fracking for short. This involves using water and acid to dissolve these rocks in order to release natural gas for capturing. Natural gas can also be converted into liquid, providing a cleaner energy source compared to the previous two fossil fuels.

An Unhealthy Addiction

According to Our World in Data (2019), a whopping 84% of our energy and 64% of our electricity came from fossil fuels alone. We rely on fossil fuels for so many of our basic needs like cooking, heating, fuel, lights, and so much more. If we stopped using them, society as a whole would collapse unless we can find some way to lessen our dependence.

PETRON

Petron is by far the largest oil refining company in the Philippines. Clocking in at 30 terminals, 32 storage facilities and over 2,400 service stations, Petron is a major provider of fuel and oil to supply our country's ever growing demand. They are the pillar of Philippine transportation.

SHELL

Shell is a British international oil and gas company that operates in over 70 countries worldwide. With 82,000 employees, they are able to produce an outstanding 3.2 million barrels of oil per day. It is for this reason that they are in direct competition with Petron in the Philippines.

For its entire history, fossil fuel consumption in the Philippines has continued to increase at a rapid rate. In fact, our consumption has nearly doubled in the past 2 decades alone, reaching a peak of nearly 500 terrawatts. However, this has both good and bad consequences. The good news is that more fossil fuels means more energy to fuel the development and economic growth of our country. The more developed we are, the higher our quality of life. The bad news is that more fossil fuels also means more carbon emitted. This leads to a phenomenon known as global warming which can have disastrous effects for our nation if left unchecked. In an ironic twist of fate, fossil fuels, the same thing we used to industrialize and advance our society to where it is now, may be the one to end it in the future.

Some Final Thoughts

To sum things up, fossil fuels are formed from ancient plants and animals that have been heated and pressurized over the years. The energy and carbon stored within them are released when they are burned, making fossil fuels both a useful source of electricity and a harmful source of greenhouse gases. Our reliance on fossil fuels has caused demand to skyrocket, exacerbating both climate change and global warming due to carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.
